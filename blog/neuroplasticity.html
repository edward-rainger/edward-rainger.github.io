Neuroplasticity and Growth Mindset

Neuroplasticity

Neuroplasticity, also known as brain plasticity, describes the brain's capacity to restructure itself by creating new neural pathways. This capability enables neurons (the brain's nerve cells) to adapt to injuries and new circumstances, thereby adjusting their functions accordingly. Here are some ways we can increase or harness Neuroplasticity:

  • Engage in activities that challenge your brain regularly, e.g learning a new instrument or taking up a new sport.
  • Embrace lifelong learning and seek out novel experiences that stimulate your brain.
  • Regular exercise has been shown to enhance neuroplasticity.
  • Quality sleep is crucial for brain health and facilitates neural repair and growth.
  • Interacting with others stimulates cognitive function and can support brain health.

Growth Mindset

Growth mindset refers to the belief that abilities and intelligence can be developed through dedication and hard work. Individuals with a growth mindset embrace challenges and view effort as a path to mastery. This is quite the opposite of fixed mindset (close minded), where abilities are seen as “what you’re born with” and unchangeable.

Growth mindset is relevant because it fosters resilience, learning, and personal development. In educational and professional settings, holding a growth mindset can lead to greater achievement and fulfilment. Throughout my learning journey at DevAcademy I will definitely need to incorporate a growth mindset. Each day I will have to struggle with challenges and view them as opportunities to grow , set realistic goals and work towards them, seek feedback and respond to it.
