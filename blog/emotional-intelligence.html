Emotional Intelligence (EQ)

What is Emotional Intelligence?

Emotional intelligence also referred to as emotional quotient (EQ), is the ability to recognize, understand, manage, and effectively express one's own emotions, as well as the ability to perceive, interpret, and respond to the emotions of others.

It involves a set of skills that enable us to navigate social interactions, manage relationships, and make decisions based on emotional awareness and empathy.

The set of skills include:

  • Self-awareness: The ability to recognise and understand one's own emotions.
  • Self-regulation: The capacity to manage and control one's emotional reactions, impulses, and behaviours, even in stressful or challenging situations.
  • Motivation: The drive to pursue goals with energy and persistence, despite setbacks, and the ability to delay gratification for long-term rewards.
  • Empathy: The skill of understanding and considering others' emotions, feelings, and perspectives, which facilitates effective communication and relationship-building.
  • Social skills: The ability to manage and navigate social interactions, communicate effectively, influence others positively, and resolve conflicts constructively.

How is EQ different to IQ?

Emotional Intelligence (EQ) and Intelligence Quotient (IQ) are two aspects of human intelligence.

IQ evaluates cognitive skills like logical thinking, problem-solving, and language abilities, crucial for academic and analytical tasks, contributing to academic and career success.

In contrast, EQ encompasses emotional and social abilities essential for managing emotions, building relationships, and navigating social situations effectively. Skills such as self-awareness, self-control, empathy, and interpersonal communication are pivotal for personal well-being and effective leadership.

Both EQ and IQ complement each other in personal and professional achievement. Integrating emotional intelligence with cognitive skills improves decision-making, enhances teamwork, and facilitates clear communication. Recognising the significance of both enables a well-rounded approach to growth.

Why is EQ important?

Emotional intelligence is essential in various aspects of life, including personal relationships, leadership, teamwork, and professional success. It enables individuals to build stronger connections with others, make informed decisions based on emotional insights, and adapt to diverse social and cultural environments. Developing emotional intelligence involves self-reflection, practice, and learning from experiences. It can be cultivated through training, coaching, and mindfulness practices that enhance emotional awareness and regulation skills. Understanding and applying emotional intelligence can lead to improved communication, enhanced interpersonal relationships, better stress management, and overall well-being.
