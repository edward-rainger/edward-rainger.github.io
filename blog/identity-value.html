Identity, Values, Strengths

Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision.

Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.

I think an ethical decision I make each day is to be honest. Due to some troubles with addiction I haven’t always told the truth and it makes it very hard for people to trust you going forward. It becomes a habit and a lot of the time you start to believe your own lies. When you make the decision to tell the truth, a few things happen, you feel better even if the truth hurts, other people will rely on you more often and you wont have to build off that lie. The values of integrity and responsibility will always give you a long term gain vs a short term fix.

Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity.

Growing up in a split-parent household with limited contact with my father and being surrounded by four sisters, my upbringing and the people around me profoundly shaped my values and identity. As a young person, I was naturally driven to achieve and spent a lot of time outside the house. However, around the age of 16, I began to rebel and became somewhat rebellious and neglectful of my studies. Despite these challenges, certain core values remained steadfast throughout my journey. Kindness, empathy, and the importance of making the right choices were values instilled in me from a young age. However they are still very much still shaping today and I am enjoying the EQ process of this course.

Identify your strengths and how they will support you during your learning journey.

One of my key strengths is adaptability. I thrive in new environments and quickly adjust to new challenges and technologies. This will be help during my learning journey as I am brand new to tech and understand that is an evolving industry. Additionally, my strong problem-solving skills enable me to approach problems creatively. These strengths will allow me to navigate uncertainties and hopefully contribute to constant growth.

Evaluate your limitations in terms of your learning and career development. How might these affect your learning journey?

While I enjoy a hands-on learning environment, I sometimes struggle with maintaining focus on repetitive tasks. This could potentially affect my learning journey by requiring me to implement strategies like breaking tasks into smaller steps or varying study methods to stay engaged. Additionally, my perfectionism tendencies might occasionally lead to spending excessive time on details rather than focusing on broader learning objectives.

Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension.

Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.

While fundraising for Movember one year, there was conflicting ideas on fundraising strategies which led to tension among my friends. Initially, we tried to discuss and compromise, but egos came out. Looking back, I see that setting clear goals and expectations upfront, rather than during a project, would have prevented this from happening.
